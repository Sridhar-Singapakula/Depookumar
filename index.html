Depoo Kumar

Assistant Professor

Metallurgical Engineering and Material Sciences

Indian Institute of Technology Bombay

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Metallurgical Engineering and Materials Science at IIT Bombay, with a strong academic foundation that includes a dual degree (B.Tech. & M.Tech.) from IIT Bombay (2008-2013) and an M.S. and Ph.D. in Materials Science and Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburg (2014-2018). During my time at Carnegie Mellon (since August 2014), I conducted extensive research on "Experimental and Modeling Studies on Kinetics of Cleanliness during Liquid Steel Processing." My research interests lie in steelmaking and casting, refractory interactions, additive manufacturing, and metal recycling, with a focus on thermodynamics, kinetics, and mass transport in pyrometallurgical processes. Through my teaching, research, and collaborations, I aim to drive innovation in materials science and engineering.

To know more about my research, please see the research page, and to know about our research group, see the group page

I have prior industry and research experience as a Post Graduate Management Trainee at L&T Special Steels and Heavy Forgings (2013–2014), where I worked on metallurgical testing of heavy forgings, failure analysis, and experimental modeling of quenching processes. Additionally, I interned at the Graduate Institute of Ferrous Technology, POSTECH, South Korea, focusing on the microstructure-property relationship in hot-rolled steel (Summer 2012)
