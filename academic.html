MM218: Process Kinetics and Transport (UG)

This advanced course focuses on the fundamental principles and applications of process kinetics and transport phenomena. Designed for students aspiring to delve into research in chemical engineering and allied disciplines, the course builds upon foundational knowledge to explore cutting-edge topics in reaction kinetics and transport processes. Key areas of focus include mass and energy balances, chemical reaction kinetics (homogeneous and heterogeneous), rate theories, and advanced transport phenomena involving fluid flow, heat, and mass transfer. The course emphasizes understanding and modeling complex systems such as reactions in porous solids, packed bed reactors, and transport in boundary layers.

MM624: Advanced Concepts in Steel Making (PG)

This course covers advanced metallurgical thermodynamics, reactor models, and process dynamics, focusing on modern steelmaking and continuous casting. Topics include deoxidation, alloy steelmaking, defect analysis, and heat transfer. Emphasis is placed on physical and mathematical modeling, process control, and recent advancements in metallurgy, preparing students for research in this field.

MM659: Transport Phenomena (PG)

This course reviews fundamentals of fluid flow, heat transfer, and mass transfer, with an introduction to turbulent flow phenomena and phase change heat transfer. It explores detailed applications of transport phenomena in materials processing, equipping students with the foundational knowledge needed for advanced research in materials engineering.

MM204: Transport Phenomena (UG)

This course covers space lattices, crystal structures, and crystallographic directions and planes. Topics include defects, grain boundaries, silicate and polymer structures, solid solutions, and intermetallic compounds. It also explores binary and ternary phase diagrams of ferrous and non-ferrous alloys, linking them to microstructural development for materials research.

MM755: Kinetics of High Temperature Processes (PG)

This course covers space lattices, crystal structures, and crystallographic directions and planes. Topics include defects, grain boundaries, silicate and polymer structures, solid solutions, and intermetallic compounds. It also explores binary and ternary phase diagrams of ferrous and non-ferrous alloys, linking them to microstructural development for materials research.

MM312: Heat Treatment Laboratory (UG-LAB)

This lab course focuses on heat treatment processes for steels, including annealing, normalizing, hardening, tempering, and thermochemical treatments like carburizing and boriding. Experiments cover hardenability measurement (Jominy test), effects of cooling media, and quantitative metallography of ceramics, composites, and steels, providing hands-on experience in analyzing microstructures and evaluating material properties.
